What is C-TPAT?

Learn about the incentive based partnership that ensures security of supply chains

C-TPAT (Customs-Trade Partnership Against Terrorism) is a partnership between the United States Government and private sector companies to ensure supply chain security.

Introduced in 2001, the Customs-Trade Partnership Against Terrorism is a voluntary, incentive based partnership between the United States government and the private sector. C-TPAT offers participants several benefits, including reduced inspections and penalties, C-TPAT account manager, mutual recognition recognition and competetive advantages.

The purpose of the C-TPAT program is to "prevent terrorists and/or terrorist weapons from entering the United States by partnering with members of the trade community to enhance the security of their ointerntional supply chains, with priority focus on foreign manufactureres through CBP clearance process".
