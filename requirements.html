C-TPAT Requirements

9 Aspects of Supply Chain Security with C-TPAT

CTPAT Requirements:

Risk Assessment Process:

Cargo Flow

Need to create a cargo map from origin to destination for all your shipments from supplier to customer.

Threat Assessment

You need to provide a list of all countries you import from and measure risk of each country

Vulnerability

A questionaire needs to be sent out to each of your suppliers and measure the risk of their facility

Action Plan

An action plan needs to be created to follow up the implementation of actions for each suppliers

Document Support

You need evidence/documents/logs to prove actions have been implemented

Business Partners:

Supplier Selection Process

Suppliers must follow C-TPAT requirements by completing annual questionnaires

Annual Questionnaire and Agreement

Each year, all suppliers and transportation carriers need to complete a security questionnaire and sign a vendor agreement

Implementation

Business partners must prove implementation of security plan with documentation.

Container Security:

7-Point Inspection or 17 Point Trailer Inspection

C-TPAT requires all incoming and outgoing containers and trailers to undergo a 7-Point inspection

Inspect the Bumper, Motor/Engine ,Tires, Cab Floor, Fuel Tank, Storage Compartments, Air Tanks, Drive Shafts, Fifth Wheel, Outside/Undercarriage, Trailer Floor, Inside Doors, Side Walls, Ceiling Roof, Front Wall, Exhaust, Seals

All containers going to the United States must have a high security seal. Make sure toView seal & container locking hardware, Verify seal number, Tug on seal to make sure it's on right, Twist and turn seal to make sure it doesn't unscrew. Look for scratches, burn marks, fresh grease, fresh paint, silicone, fiberglass patchwork, and odor masking substances.

Container Storage

C-TPAT requires all containers to be stored securely. This involves checking the integrity of seals, verifying seal numbers and reporting compromised seals.

Cargo Information

C-TPAT requires information on cargo is accurate, legible, complete and on time. Report and investigate all differences.

Physical Access Controls:

Employee Identification

All employees must wear visible photo ID badges, and report unauthorized persons found in any facility.

Limit employee access per duty

Employees must given only enough clearance to complete their job. This ensures that those interacting with cargo are known. This is very important for seal affixing.

Parking

No parking in cargo areas.

Physical Security Controls:

Building Integrity

Regular building integrity checks. Doors, gates and windows need to be equipped with locks.

Security and Surveillance

Facilities must have adequate lighting. Alarm systems and video surveillance systems are reccomended.

Gates, Fences and Keys

Facilities must have perimeter gates and fences, alongside control on issuance of door keys.

Personnel Security:

Background Checks

All employees must have had background checks; this includes verifying previous employment history and checking references

Periodic Checks

Some employees to require periodic checks dependent on the cause/sensitivity of position.

Terminated Employees

ID badges, facility and system access must be revoked from terminated employees.

Procedural Security:

Cargo Information

Cargo information should be accurate, complete, legible and on time

Information on Cargo

Purchase order should match commercial invoice

Reporting

All discrepancies should be reported and investigated

Security and Awareness Training :

Annual Employee Training

All employees must receive annual training on C-TPAT

Shipping, Receiving and Mail Employees

Employees in shipping, receiving and mail must receive additional 7 point/14 point training.

I.T. Security:

Server

Servers must be secure, have a firewall and the company has control over the server

Computers

All computers have individual passwords, as well as restricted desktop access.

System Abuse

All abuses of the system call for disciplinary actions.
